MileSplit Live - Redesign

MileSplit Live helps athletes, coaches, and fans keep up with track & field meets in real-time. The product belongs to MileSplit, which is a track and cross country hub that publishes a variety of data and editorial content including meet results, rankings, and articles.

MileSplit Live was doing well, but the company recognized the potential to improve the aesthetics and usability of the product. As an in-house designer, I was tasked to help realize this potential.

Understanding the Problem

I began the project by talking to current customers about how they used the product. These conversations revealed that the product was meeting their basic needs but was generally lacking depth.

Energizing The Brand

The product had a design system that was functional, but uninspiring. So, I enhanced and expanded the system to better fit the energy of track & field.

Final Designs

Improving Navigability

Although the product contained a treasure trove of valuable data and content, customers were struggling to find and discover all that was offered. To address this challenge, we made improvements to the event list and filters, and designed a new search feature.

Showcasing Rankings and Records

In track and cross country, rankings and records are essential for keeping participants and fans up-to-date with the latest in the sport. We catered to this interest by designing ways to make rankings and records information more accessible and engaging.

Optimizing Scan-ability

Finish times, splits, marks, heights, and distances are the core offering of the product. To ensure these results were presented in the most engaging and consumable way possible, I redesigned the tables in the product to be more visually appealing and scannable.

Integrating Athlete Profiles

Athletes are the heart of the sport, and behind each record and finishing time is a story. To provide a more comprehensive experience, we introduced athlete profiles to the product, enabling customers to delve into these stories.

Results

We launched this redesign alongside efforts from multiple teams at MileSplit, including engineering, rights acquisition, and others. As a result, we saw a significant increase across several metrics.
