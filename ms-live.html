MileSplit Live - Redesign

MileSplit is a hub for track & field and cross country enthusiasts, offering a range of data and editorial content including meet results, state rankings, and articles covering the latest happenings in the sports. Additionally, they offer a product called MileSplit Live which enables athletes, coaches, and fans to keep up with the action at track and cross country meets in real-time.

Despite its success, MileSplit Live had been scarcely updated since its original launch. Recognizing the product's potential for improvement, I was tasked with enhancing its aesthetics and usability.

Understanding the Problem

I began the project by talking to current customers about how they used the product. These conversations revealed that the product was meeting their basic needs but was generally lacking depth.

Energizing The Brand

The product had a design system that was functional, but uninspiring. So, I enhanced and expanded the system to better fit the energy of track & field.

Final Designs

Improving Navigability

Although the product contained a treasure trove of valuable data and content, customers were struggling to find and discover all that was offered. To address this challenge, we made improvements to the event list and filters, and introduced new functionality such as search.

Showcasing Rankings and Records

In the world of track and cross country, rankings and records are essential for keeping participants and fans up-to-date with the latest in the sport. We catered to this interest by designing ways to make rankings and records information more accessible and engaging.

Optimizing Scan-ability

The core offering of the product is the results from the events, such as finish times, splits, marks, heights, and distances. To ensure these results were presented in the most engaging and consumable way possible, I redesigned the tables in the product to be more visually appealing and scannable.

Integrating Athlete Profiles

Athletes are the heart of the sport, and behind each record and finishing time is a story. To provide a more comprehensive experience, we introduced athlete profiles to the product, enabling customers to delve into these stories.

Results

We launched this redesign alongside efforts from multiple teams at MileSplit, including engineering, rights acquisition, and others. As a result, we saw a significant increase across several metrics.
