FloSports - Schedules & Scores

FloSports offers coverage and live streaming for a wide range of sports, from wrestling to racing, accessible via various platforms including iOS and Android. The apps feature a schedule which allows customers to stay informed about upcoming events, games, and matches for their favorite sports.

The schedule feature was sufficient in the early days of the business, but FloSports was introducing thousands of new events to their offering, and had aspirations of eventually providing additional features such as live scores.

I was tasked with redesigning the schedule to better support the influx of new events and lay the groundwork for the future.

Exploring the Possibilities

I began the project by conducting some high level exploration of potential solutions to ensure that all options were thoroughly considered. Following this exploration, I vetted the ideas with stakeholders and converged on a direction that we felt was promising.

Validating the Designs

After settling on a direction, I proceeded to create a prototype and user test the designs. The testing went great and we were able to catch and address some issues before moving to the development stage.

Final Designs

Browsing by Date

Customers frequently use date as a way to categorize events. So, we introduced features that let customers seamlessly navigate through time, whether they’re interested in what’s happening tomorrow, or what’s happening next month.

Event List

The list components were designed to work for all types of sports, and to disclose just the right amount of information. Our goal was to create a list that felt uncluttered and scannable, while also ensuring that all critical information was included.

Filtering and Searching

In order to help customers drill down to a particular subset of events, we introduced filters. We also implemented a search feature that lets customers quickly get to specific events.

Future Concepts

Integrating scores into the schedule was not within scope for the first phase of the project. However, we knew this was something our customers would love, and that we eventually wanted to do. So, I did some exploration to envision what that integration might look like.

Results

We saw great feedback from the user testing sessions, followed by positive increases in a few key metrics after launching. More importantly, we laid the groundwork for future, high-impact improvements.
